# French Bulldog Dental Care

Dental health is one of the most overlooked aspects of French Bulldog care. By age 3, most Frenchies show signs of dental disease.

## Why Dental Care Matters

Poor dental health leads to more than bad breath. Bacteria from the mouth enters the bloodstream, damaging the heart, kidneys, and liver. Regular dental care can add 2-3 years to your Frenchie's life.

## Common Dental Issues in Frenchies

- **Plaque and tartar buildup** — Frenchies are prone due to their compact mouths
- **Periodontal disease** — gum inflammation and infection
- **Tooth crowding** — teeth may overlap, trapping food
- **Baby teeth retention** — some puppies retain baby teeth that need removal
- **Bad breath (halitosis)** — first sign of dental problems

## Daily Dental Care Routine

### Brushing
1. Use a dog-specific toothbrush (finger brush works well for Frenchies)
2. Use dog-safe toothpaste — NEVER human toothpaste (toxic)
3. Brush in small circles along the gum line
4. Focus on the back teeth where plaque accumulates most
5. Aim for daily, but 3-4 times per week is a good minimum

### Dental Chews and Toys
- Look for VOHC (Veterinary Oral Health Council) approved products
- Avoid hard chews (antlers, bones, nylon) — can break teeth
- Rubber toys with ridges help clean teeth during play
- Dental treats with enzymes help reduce plaque

## Professional Dental Cleanings

Your vet should perform annual dental checkups with professional cleaning under anesthesia. This includes scaling, polishing, and X-rays to check below the gum line.

## Warning Signs

- Bad breath that persists after brushing
- Yellow or brown tartar on teeth
- Red, swollen, or bleeding gums
- Dropping food or chewing on one side
- Pawing at the mouth
- Loose or missing teeth

## Diet for Dental Health

- High-quality dry kibble helps scrape teeth
- Dental-specific prescription diets available
- Raw carrots and apple slices (supervised) as crunchy treats
- Avoid soft, sticky foods that cling to teeth

Start dental care early — a puppy accustomed to tooth brushing will accept it for life.
