# French Bulldog Exercise Needs

French Bulldogs have unique exercise requirements due to their brachycephalic (flat-faced) anatomy. Here is the complete guide.

## Daily Exercise Requirements

Adult French Bulldogs need about **20-30 minutes of moderate exercise per day**, split into two 10-15 minute sessions. Puppies need less — about 5 minutes per month of age.

## Best Exercises for Frenchies

- **Short walks** — 10-15 minutes, morning and evening
- **Indoor play** — fetch, tug-of-war in air-conditioned rooms
- **Mental stimulation** — puzzle toys, snuffle mats, training sessions
- **Swimming** — only with a life jacket and supervision

## Warning Signs of Overexertion

Stop immediately if you see: excessive panting, drooling, staggering, blue or pale gums, vomiting, or collapse. Frenchies cannot cool themselves efficiently and can overheat quickly.

## Exercise by Weather

| Condition | Recommendation |
|-----------|---------------|
| Below 75°F (24°C) | Normal walks OK |
| 75-85°F (24-29°C) | Short walks, avoid midday |
| Above 85°F (29°C) | Indoor play only |
| Cold below 40°F (4°C) | Short walks with sweater |

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. Over-exercising — Frenchies are not jogging partners
2. Exercise in hot weather — leading cause of heatstroke
3. Not enough mental stimulation — can lead to destructive behavior
4. Letting them overdo it at the dog park

Always prioritize quality over quantity. A well-exercised Frenchie is a happy Frenchie.
