# French Bulldog Rescue

Thousands of French Bulldogs need homes every year. Rescue organizations across the country work tirelessly to rehab and rehome Frenchies in need.

## Why Frenchies End Up in Rescue

- Owner health issues or financial hardship
- Owner did not research the breed's needs
- Behavioral issues (often from lack of training)
- Health problems the owner cannot afford
- Breed-specific legislation forcing relocation
- Surrender from puppy mills or backyard breeders

## Major French Bulldog Rescue Organizations

- **French Bulldog Rescue Network (FBRN)** — National network covering the US and Canada
- **Short-Strides French Bulldog Rescue** — Focuses on senior and special-needs Frenchies
- **Once Upon a French Bulldog** — Covers Southern US
- **Left Paw Rescue** — California-based
- **Frenchie Rescue** — Multiple locations, focuses on medical cases
- **Local breed-specific rescues** — Search for your state or region

## The Adoption Process

### 1. Application
Most rescues require a detailed application covering your home, lifestyle, and experience.

### 2. Reference Check
Rescues will call your vet and personal references.

### 3. Home Visit
A volunteer inspects your home for safety — fenced yard, hazards, living conditions.

### 4. Meet and Greet
If approved, you meet the Frenchie to see if it is a good match.

### 6. Trial Period

## What to Expect from a Rescue Frenchie

Rescued Frenchies often have medical or behavioral issues. Be prepared for:
- Unknown medical history
- Possible heartworm, dental disease, or skin conditions
- Fear or anxiety from past trauma
- House training regression
- Adjustment period of 3-6 months

## Fostering

Fostering is a great way to help without a permanent commitment. Rescues always need foster homes. They cover all medical costs — you provide love, food, and a safe space.

## Tips for Successful Adoption

1. Be patient — the right match may take months
2. Consider senior Frenchies — they deserve golden years too
3. Budget for medical care — rescues often need ongoing treatment
4. Invest in training — many rescue dogs benefit from professional help
5. Give them time — the 3-3-3 rule: 3 days to decompress, 3 weeks to learn routine, 3 months to feel at home

Adopting a rescue Frenchie is challenging and incredibly rewarding. You are not just saving a life — you are gaining a devoted companion.
