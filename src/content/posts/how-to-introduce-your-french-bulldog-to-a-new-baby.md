# How to Introduce Your French Bulldog to a New Baby

## Preparing Your Frenchie Before the Baby Arrives
Start preparing at least 2-3 months before your due date. Gradually change routines so your Frenchie adjusts before the baby arrives.

### Step 1: Adjust Your Routine Early
Frenchies thrive on routine. If walks or feeding times will change with the baby, shift them gradually beforehand.

### Step 2: Introduce Baby Sounds and Smells
Play recordings of baby sounds (crying, cooing) at low volume while giving treats. Bring home a baby blanket from the hospital for your Frenchie to sniff before you bring the baby home.

### Step 3: Practice Boundaries
Teach your Frenchie to stay on their bed or mat while you hold a doll. Reward calm behavior.

## The First Introduction
Keep the first meeting calm and controlled. Let your Frenchie sniff the baby briefly, then reward calm behavior.

## Safety Rules
- Never leave your Frenchie unsupervised with an infant
- Give your Frenchie a safe space away from the baby
- Maintain your Frenchie exercise and attention schedule
- Watch for signs of stress (hiding, lip licking, tucked tail)

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
1. Ignoring your Frenchie when the baby arrives
2. Allowing face-to-face contact - babies grab, Frenchies react
3. Punishing normal curiosity - let them investigate calmly

## The Positive Outcome
Most French Bulldogs become loving protectors of their human siblings.
