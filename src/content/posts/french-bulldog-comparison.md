# French Bulldog vs Pug vs Boston Terrier

French Bulldogs, Pugs, and Boston Terriers are often compared. All three are small, flat-faced companion dogs, but they have distinct differences.

## Quick Comparison

| Feature | French Bulldog | Pug | Boston Terrier |
|---------|---------------|-----|----------------|
| Weight | 16-28 lbs | 14-18 lbs | 12-25 lbs |
| Height | 11-13 inches | 10-13 inches | 15-17 inches |
| Lifespan | 10-12 years | 12-15 years | 11-13 years |
| Energy Level | Low-Moderate | Low | Moderate |
| Shedding | Moderate | High | Low |
| Trainability | Stubborn | Easy | Moderate |
| Cost | -$ | $- |  |

## French Bulldog

**Pros**: Quiet, affectionate, sturdy, good with children, low exercise needs, alert watchdog
**Cons**: Expensive, prone to health issues, stubborn, cannot tolerate heat, lots of vet bills

**Best for**: Apartment dwellers, first-time owners, families, people who want a Velcro dog

## Pug

**Pros**: Affectionate, comical, good with children and other pets, adaptable, lower purchase cost
**Cons**: Heavy shedding, snoring, prone to obesity, eye problems, wrinkles need daily cleaning

**Best for**: Families with children, multi-pet households, people who want a playful clown

## Boston Terrier

**Pros**: Active, intelligent, low shedding, longer legs (less breathing issues), easier to train, healthier overall
**Cons**: Higher energy, can be more stubborn, prone to eye injuries, sensitive to cold

**Best for**: Active owners, people who want a dog that can keep up, those concerned about health issues

## Health Comparison

While all three are brachycephalic, the severity varies:
- **Boston Terrier**: Least affected — longer muzzle
- **Pug**: Moderate — compact but less extreme
- **French Bulldog**: Most affected — shortest muzzle, highest BOAS risk

## Exercise Needs

| Breed | Daily Exercise |
|-------|---------------|
| Boston Terrier | 45-60 minutes |
| French Bulldog | 20-30 minutes |
| Pug | 20-30 minutes |

## Which Should You Choose?

**Choose a French Bulldog if**: You want a quiet, affectionate, low-energy companion and can handle higher costs and health issues.

**Choose a Pug if**: You want a happy-go-lucky, family-friendly dog with a lower purchase price and do not mind heavy shedding.

**Choose a Boston Terrier if**: You want a more active, healthier brachycephalic breed with lower shedding and easier trainability.

All three are wonderful breeds. Your choice depends on your lifestyle, budget, and tolerance for health issues.
