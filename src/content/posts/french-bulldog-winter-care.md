# French Bulldog Winter Care

French Bulldogs have short coats and low body fat, making them sensitive to cold weather. Here is how to keep your Frenchie warm and safe during winter.

## Why Frenchies Get Cold Easily

- Short, single-layer coat with minimal insulation
- Low body fat percentage
- Small body size with high surface-to-volume ratio
- Brachycephalic airway issues worsen in cold, dry air

## Temperature Guidelines

| Temperature | Risk Level | Action Needed |
|-------------|-----------|---------------|
| Above 45°F (7°C) | Low | Normal walks |
| 32-45°F (0-7°C) | Moderate | Jacket recommended, shorter walks |
| 20-32°F (-6 to 0°C) | High | Warm coat essential, limit to 10 min |
| Below 20°F (-6°C) | Extreme | Bathroom breaks only, no walks |

## Winter Essentials

### Clothing
- Insulated winter coat or puffer jacket
- Waterproof booties (for snow and salt)
- Sweater for indoor wear in drafty homes

### Home Care
- Orthopedic bed with warm fleece lining
- Heated pet bed or pad (low voltage, chew-resistant cord)
- Humidifier to combat dry air that irritates breathing
- Keep home between 68-72°F (20-22°C)

## Winter Health Risks

### Hypothermia Signs
Shivering, lethargy, weak pulse, shallow breathing, pale gums. Warm your Frenchie gradually — never use hot water or heating pads directly.

### Paw Care
- Salt and de-icers burn paw pads — rinse paws after walks
- Apply paw balm before going outside
- Trim fur between paw pads to prevent ice balls

### Dry Skin
Winter air causes dry, flaky skin. Add fish oil to their diet and use a humidifier.

## Winter Exercise

Indoor alternatives for cold days:
- Tug-of-war in the hallway
- Hide and seek with treats
- Stair climbing (supervised)
- Indoor fetch with soft toys
- Puzzle toys and training sessions

A warm Frenchie is a safe Frenchie. When in doubt, keep them inside with a cozy bed and a chew toy.
