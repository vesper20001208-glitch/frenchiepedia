# French Bulldog Breeding

Breeding French Bulldogs requires specialized knowledge and significant resources. Unlike many breeds, Frenchies almost always require artificial insemination and C-sections.

## The Reality of Frenchie Breeding

- Most Frenchies cannot mate naturally due to physical conformation
- Over 80% require artificial insemination (AI)
- Over 90% require C-section deliveries
- Average litter size: 3-4 puppies (smallest of all dog breeds)
- High veterinary costs: ,000-,000+ per litter

## Health Testing Before Breeding

Responsible breeders test for:
- **BOAS grading** — airway function assessment
- **Hip evaluation** — OFA or PennHIP
- **Patellar luxation** — knee stability
- **Heart evaluation** — cardiac clearance by a cardiologist
- **Eye examination** — CERF or ACVO
- **DNA tests** — for breed-specific genetic conditions

## Breeding Timeline

**Females should not be bred before:** 2 years of age (after full maturity)
**Recommended breeding frequency:** No more than 3 litters in a lifetime, always with recovery breaks

## The Breeding Process

### 1. Progesterone Testing
Blood tests every 2-3 days to determine optimal breeding time

### 2. Artificial Insemination
Fresh, chilled, or frozen semen inseminated by a veterinarian

### 3. Pregnancy Confirmation
Ultrasound at 25-30 days or hormone test at 22-25 days

### 4. Pregnancy Care
- Switch to high-quality puppy food at week 6
- Limit exercise and jumping
- X-rays at day 56-58 to count puppies (minimum 2 needed for safe C-section)

### 5. Scheduled C-Section
Typically scheduled for day 60-61 of gestation

### 6. Puppy Care
- Immediate warming and stimulation
- First vet check within 24 hours
- Deworming at 2, 4, 6, 8 weeks
- Vaccinations at 6-8 weeks

## Ethical Considerations

- Never breed a Frenchie with severe BOAS symptoms
- Never breed for "rare" colors (blue, lilac, merle) — these come with health risks
- Always have homes lined up before breeding
- Be prepared to keep puppies if homes fall through
- Health-test the parents before every breeding
