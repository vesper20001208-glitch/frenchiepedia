# French Bulldog Weight Management

Over 40% of French Bulldogs are overweight or obese. Carrying extra weight dramatically increases health risks for this already vulnerable breed.

## Ideal Weight Range

| Gender | Healthy Weight Range |
|--------|---------------------|
| Male | 20-28 lbs (9-13 kg) |
| Female | 16-24 lbs (7-11 kg) |

## How to Check If Your Frenchie Is Overweight

**Rib check**: Run your hands along their ribcage. You should feel ribs easily with a thin layer of fat, like touching the back of your hand.

**Waist check**: Looking from above, there should be a visible waist behind the ribs.

**Belly check**: From the side, the belly should tuck up behind the ribcage.

## Health Risks of Obesity

- **Exacerbates BOAS** — extra neck fat compresses airways further
- **Joint problems** — hip dysplasia, arthritis, IVDD
- **Heart disease** — increased strain on the heart
- **Diabetes** — significantly higher risk
- **Heatstroke risk** — overweight Frenchies overheat faster
- **Reduced lifespan** — obesity reduces lifespan by up to 2.5 years

## Weight Loss Plan

### Step 1: Vet Consultation
Determine target weight and rule out medical causes (thyroid issues).

### Step 2: Measure Food
Most owners overfeed by 25-50%. Use a measuring cup or kitchen scale.

**Daily calorie guide**: Adult Frenchie needs 350-500 calories per day depending on activity level.

### Step 3: Reduce Treats
Treats should be no more than 10% of daily calories. Use healthier alternatives:
- Frozen green beans
- Carrot sticks
- Cucumber slices
- Blueberries
- Ice cubes

### Step 4: Increase Exercise
Start slow — 5-10 minute walks, twice daily. Gradually increase as fitness improves. Include indoor play and mental stimulation.

### Step 5: Monthly Weigh-Ins
Track progress weekly. Healthy loss is 1-3% of body weight per month.

## Feeding Schedule

- Adults: 2 meals per day (not free-feeding)
- Measure each meal precisely
- Use slow feeder bowls to prevent gulping
- Wait 30 minutes after exercise before feeding

## Foods to Avoid

Fatty meats, table scraps, high-carb treats, human junk food, cheese (high fat), peanut butter in large amounts (high calorie).

A lean Frenchie is a healthy Frenchie. Every pound matters for their quality of life.
