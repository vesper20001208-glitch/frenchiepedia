# Understanding Your French Bulldog Communication

## The Frenchie Language
French Bulldogs are expressive communicators using sounds, body language, and facial expressions.

## Common Sounds
The Grunt: contentment or mild annoyance.
The Snort: excitement or impatience.
The Whine: urgent need - potty, food, or attention.
The Play Growl: fun, not aggression.
The Alert Bark: someone at the door.
The Sigh: contentment or resignation.
The Frenchie Scream: genuine distress - investigate immediately.

## Body Language
Tail up and wagging: happy and confident.
Tail down and tucked: scared or anxious.
Ears forward: interested or alert.
Ears flattened: scared or submissive.
Whale eye (white showing): anxious or uncomfortable.

## The Frenchie Stare
An intense stare usually means I want something - check the treat jar or leash.

## Building Better Communication
Respond consistently to signals. Reward clear communication. Never punish vocalization.

The more you listen, the better you will understand your Frenchie.
