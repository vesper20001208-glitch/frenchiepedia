# French Bulldog Intelligence

French Bulldogs have a reputation for being stubborn, but that does not mean they are not smart. Understanding how Frenchies think is the key to training them effectively.

## The Rankings

In Stanley Coren's "The Intelligence of Dogs," French Bulldogs ranked 109th out of 138 breeds in working/obedience intelligence. This means they often need 25-40 repetitions to learn a new command.

## Three Types of Intelligence

### Instinctive Intelligence
Frenchies were bred as companion dogs, not working dogs. Their instinctive intelligence focuses on human bonding, reading emotions, and communication — areas where they excel.

### Adaptive Intelligence
This is their ability to solve problems independently. Frenchies score high here — they learn how to open cabinets, manipulate their owners, and find creative ways to get what they want.

### Working/Obedience Intelligence
This is where they rank lower. They are less motivated to please humans unconditionally and prefer to do things their way.

## The Frenchie Mindset

French Bulldogs are not less intelligent — they are differently motivated:

- **What motivates them**: Food, play, affection (in that order)
- **What does not motivate them**: Pleasing you for its own sake
- **Their philosophy**: "What is in it for me?"

## Training Methods That Work

### Positive Reinforcement
Frenchies respond best to treats and praise. Harsh methods shut them down.

### Short Sessions
5-10 minute sessions, 2-3 times per day. Frenchies lose interest quickly.

### High-Value Treats
Save special treats (cheese, chicken, freeze-dried liver) for training sessions.

### Consistency
Use the same words and hand signals every time. Everyone in the household must use the same commands.

## What Frenchies Excel At

- Reading human emotions
- Learning routines and schedules
- Manipulating their owners for treats
- Opening cabinets and doors
- Finding comfortable sleeping spots
- Communicating their needs (standing by the treat jar, bringing their leash)

## The Stubborn Myth

Frenchies are not stubborn — they are selective. They understand what you want; they are deciding whether to comply. This is adaptive intelligence, not stupidity.

## Intelligence Test

To see how smart your Frenchie is:
1. Hide a treat under a cup and see if they move the cup
2. Teach a new trick and count the repetitions
3. See how quickly they learn to open a treat-dispensing puzzle toy
4. Observe whether they develop unique communication signals

Most Frenchie owners are surprised by how clever their dogs really are. They just choose when to show it.
