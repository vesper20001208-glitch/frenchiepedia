# French Bulldog Summer Care

Summer is the most dangerous season for French Bulldogs. Their flat faces make them extremely vulnerable to heatstroke. Here is how to keep your Frenchie safe.

## Why Frenchies Overheat Easily

French Bulldogs have brachycephalic airway syndrome (BOAS), which means they cannot pant efficiently — their primary cooling mechanism. Combined with a thick coat and short snout, they are at extreme risk of heatstroke.

## Heatstroke Warning Signs

**Mild**: Excessive panting, drooling, restlessness
**Moderate**: Bright red gums, vomiting, diarrhea, staggering
**Severe**: Collapse, seizures, unconsciousness

## Emergency First Aid

If you suspect heatstroke:
1. Move to shade or air conditioning immediately
2. Pour cool (not cold) water over body — focus on paws, belly, head
3. Offer small amounts of water
4. Get to a vet immediately — heatstroke is fatal without treatment

## Summer Safety Tips

### Never Walk in Midday Heat
Walk only early morning (before 8 AM) or late evening (after 8 PM). Test the pavement with your hand — if it is too hot for your hand, it is too hot for their paws.

### Provide Constant Cool Water
Add ice cubes to water bowls. Consider a pet water fountain to encourage drinking.

### Create Cool Zones
- Cooling mats or beds
- Fans or air conditioning
- Access to tile or concrete floors
- Frozen treats (frozen blueberries, ice cubes with chicken broth)

### Never Leave in a Parked Car
Even with windows cracked, a car reaches 120°F+ in minutes. This kills.

## Summer Gear Checklist

- [ ] Cooling vest or bandana
- [ ] Portable water bottle with bowl
- [ ] Paw wax for hot pavement
- [ ] Sunscreen (dog-safe, for nose and ears)
- [ ] Cooling mat for home

## When to Stay Indoors

- Temperature above 85°F (29°C)
- High humidity
- Poor air quality (smoke, pollution)
- Your Frenchie is overweight, elderly, or has breathing issues

A cool Frenchie is a safe Frenchie. Never take summer risks with this breed.
