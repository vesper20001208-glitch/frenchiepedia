# French Bulldog Travel Guide

Traveling with a French Bulldog requires extra preparation due to their brachycephalic nature. Many airlines have restrictions, and car safety is essential.

## Flying with a French Bulldog

Most major airlines **ban** brachycephalic dogs from cargo holds. Your Frenchie must fly in the cabin.

### Airlines That Allow Frenchies in Cabin
- Delta (carry-on allowed, max 20 lbs with carrier)
- American Airlines (carry-on, max 20 lbs)
- United (carry-on, max 20 lbs)
- JetBlue (carry-on, max 20 lbs)
- Southwest (carry-on, max 20 lbs)

### Air Travel Tips
- Book early — only 2-4 pets per flight
- Use an airline-approved carrier with ventilation on all sides
- Line the carrier with a pee pad
- Freeze a water bowl attachment for long flights
- Get a health certificate from your vet within 10 days of travel

## Car Travel Safety

Never let your Frenchie roam freely in the car. Use a crash-tested harness or a secured carrier.

### Car Essentials Kit
- Crash-tested harness or carrier
- Portable water bowl and bottled water
- Travel bed or blanket
- Poop bags and wipes
- First aid kit
- Cooling mat for summer trips

## Road Trip Planning

### Every 2 Hours
Stop for a 10-minute break — bathroom, water, and a short walk

### Never Leave Your Frenchie Alone in the Car
Even with windows cracked, a car can reach 120°F (49°C) in minutes

## Travel Checklist

- [ ] Airline-approved carrier
- [ ] Health certificate (if flying)
- [ ] Vaccination records
- [ ] Food and treats for the trip
- [ ] Portable bowls
- [ ] Pee pads
- [ ] Favorite toy or blanket
- [ ] Leash and harness (not collar — choking hazard)
- [ ] Cooling vest or mat

With proper preparation, Frenchies can be excellent travel companions.
