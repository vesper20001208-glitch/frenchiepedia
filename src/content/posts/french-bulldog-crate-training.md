# French Bulldog Crate Training

Crate training is one of the most important skills for your French Bulldog. It provides them with a safe den and makes house training easier.

## Choosing the Right Crate

- **Size**: Large enough to stand, turn, and lie down — not bigger
- **Type**: Wire crates offer ventilation; plastic crates feel more den-like
- **Feature**: A divider panel lets you adjust space as your puppy grows

## Step-by-Step Training

### Week 1: Introduction
1. Place the crate in a busy family area
2. Keep the door open and toss treats inside
3. Feed meals near the crate entrance
4. Never force your Frenchie inside

### Week 2: Short Closed-Door Sessions
1. Call your Frenchie into the crate with a treat
2. Close the door for 1 minute while sitting nearby
3. Gradually increase to 5 minutes
4. Always use a calm release word ("okay" or "free")

### Week 3+: Building Duration
1. Practice leaving the room for short periods
2. Build up to 30 minutes, then 1 hour, then 2 hours
3. Adult Frenchies can handle 4-6 hours max

## Common Mistakes

- Using the crate as punishment — it should be their safe space
- Leaving them in too long — puppies need bathroom breaks every 2-3 hours
- Letting them out when they cry — wait for a quiet moment

## Pro Tips

- Cover the crate with a light blanket for better sleep
- Add a crate-safe bed and a chew toy
- Keep the crate near your bed at night for bonding
- Reward calm behavior inside the crate

Crate training takes patience but every Frenchie can learn to love their crate.
