# French Bulldog Puppy Essentials

Bringing home a French Bulldog puppy is exciting. Here is everything you need to prepare before your new family member arrives.

## Before the Puppy Arrives

### Puppy-Proof Your Home
- Secure electrical cords and cables
- Move toxic plants out of reach
- Install baby gates for restricted areas
- Remove small objects that could be swallowed
- Set up a designated puppy zone with pee pads

### Choose a Veterinarian
Find a vet experienced with brachycephalic breeds. Schedule the first health check within 48 hours of bringing your puppy home.

## Essential Supplies Checklist

### Sleeping Area
- Crate with divider panel (adjustable as puppy grows)
- Soft, washable dog bed or crate mat
- Fleece blanket or Snuggle Puppy (heat pack + heartbeat)
- Playpen for supervised containment

### Feeding
- High-quality puppy food (large breed or all-life-stages)
- Stainless steel or ceramic bowls (slow feeder recommended)
- Food measuring cup or kitchen scale
- Treats for training (small, soft, low-calorie)

### Walking and Outdoor
- Harness (not collar — Frenchies have fragile tracheas)
- 4-6 foot leash (no retractable leashes for puppies)
- Poop bags and dispenser
- ID tag with your phone number
- Portable water bottle with bowl

### Grooming
- Soft bristle brush
- Wrinkle wipes (unscented, dog-safe)
- Nail clippers (guillotine style or grinder)
- Dog-safe toothbrush and toothpaste
- Tear stain remover wipes
- Dog shampoo (hypoallergenic)

### Health and Safety
- Pet first aid kit
- Flea and tick prevention (vet recommended)
- Deworming medication (as directed by vet)
- Microchip registration

## Puppy-Proofing Checklist

- [ ] Electrical cords secured
- [ ] Toxic plants removed
- [ ] Chemicals and cleaners locked away
- [ ] Small objects picked up
- [ ] Trash cans with secure lids
- [ ] Shoes and clothes stored away
- [ ] Baby gates installed
- [ ] Designated potty area set up

## First 24 Hours

1. Let your puppy explore their new home at their own pace
2. Introduce the crate with treats and an open door
3. Offer water but wait a few hours before feeding
4. Take them to their potty area every 30-60 minutes
5. Keep the environment calm — no big gatherings
6. Let them sleep — puppies need 18-20 hours of sleep per day

Preparation is the key to a smooth transition. Your Frenchie puppy will thank you with years of love and companionship.
