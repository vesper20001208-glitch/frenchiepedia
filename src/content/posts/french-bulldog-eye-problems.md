# French Bulldog Eye Problems

French Bulldogs are prone to several eye conditions due to their prominent eyes and shallow eye sockets. Early detection is key to preventing vision loss.

## Cherry Eye

**What it is**: Prolapse of the third eyelid gland, appearing as a red mass in the inner corner of the eye.

**Treatment**: Surgical replacement of the gland (not removal — the gland produces essential tears). Recovery is quick with proper aftercare.

**Breeds affected**: French Bulldogs have the highest incidence of cherry eye of any breed.

## Dry Eye (Keratoconjunctivitis Sicca)

**What it is**: Insufficient tear production causing dry, irritated eyes.

**Symptoms**: Thick yellow discharge, red eyes, squinting, dull cornea.

**Treatment**: Lifelong artificial tear drops and immune-suppressing eye medication. Regular vet monitoring needed.

## Entropion

**What it is**: Eyelid rolls inward, causing eyelashes to rub against the cornea.

**Symptoms**: Squinting, excessive tearing, pawing at eyes, redness.

**Treatment**: Surgical correction to roll the eyelid outward. Often done at the same time as cherry eye surgery.

## Cataracts

**What it is**: Clouding of the eye lens, causing vision impairment.

**Symptoms**: Cloudy or bluish appearance in the eye, bumping into furniture, reluctance to navigate stairs.

**Treatment**: Surgical removal by a veterinary ophthalmologist. Most dogs regain excellent vision after surgery.

## Corneal Ulcers

Frenchies are prone due to their prominent eyes. Scratches from rough play, plants, or debris can quickly become serious ulcers.

**Emergency signs**: Squinting, blue/cloudy cornea, excessive tearing, visible wound on eye surface. See a vet immediately.

## Daily Eye Care

1. Wipe eye wrinkles daily with a damp cloth
2. Check for redness, discharge, or cloudiness
3. Trim long facial hair around eyes
4. Use dog-safe eyewash for mild irritation
5. Never use human eye drops

## When to See an Eye Specialist

- Any eye injury or ulcer
- Cherry eye that does not resolve
- Chronic dry eye
- Vision loss or cataracts
- Eye pain (squinting, pawing, avoiding light)

Most eye problems are treatable when caught early. Regular eye checks should be part of every Frenchie health routine.
