# French Bulldog Allergies

French Bulldogs are one of the most allergy-prone dog breeds. Up to 60% of Frenchies suffer from some form of allergy.

## Types of Allergies

### Food Allergies
Affect about 10% of Frenchies. Most common triggers:
- Chicken (most common)
- Beef
- Dairy
- Wheat/grains
- Eggs
- Soy

**Symptoms**: Itchy skin, ear infections, digestive issues (vomiting, diarrhea), anal gland problems.

### Environmental Allergies (Atopic Dermatitis)
Affect about 30% of Frenchies. Most common triggers:
- Dust mites
- Pollen (grass, trees, weeds)
- Mold spores
- Dander

**Symptoms**: Seasonal itching, paw licking, face rubbing, recurrent ear infections.

### Contact Allergies
- Certain fabrics (wool, synthetics)
- Cleaning products
- Flea treatments
- Plastic food bowls

**Symptoms**: Localized redness, bumps, hair loss on contact areas.

## Diagnosis

### Food Trial
Feed a novel protein or hydrolyzed protein diet for 8-12 weeks. If symptoms improve, reintroduce old foods one at a time to identify triggers.

### Allergy Testing
- **Blood test (serum IgE test)**: Screens for environmental allergens
- **Intradermal skin test**: More accurate, performed by a veterinary dermatologist

## Treatment Options

### For Food Allergies
- Switch to limited ingredient diet
- Novel protein sources (fish, rabbit, venison, duck)
- Hydrolyzed protein prescription diet
- Grain-free options (consult vet first)

### For Environmental Allergies
- Antihistamines (Benadryl, Zyrtec — vet-approved dosage)
- Omega-3 fatty acid supplements
- Medicated shampoos (chlorhexidine, ketoconazole)
- Immunotherapy (allergy shots or sublingual drops)
- Cytopoint or Apoquel injections (severe cases)

## Management Tips

1. Wipe paws after walks to remove allergens
2. Use hypoallergenic dog shampoo weekly
3. Wash bedding weekly in hot water
4. Use air purifiers with HEPA filters
5. Switch to stainless steel or ceramic bowls
6. Keep a symptom diary to identify triggers

## When to See a Dermatologist

If your Frenchie has chronic itching, recurrent ear infections, or skin infections that do not respond to basic treatment, see a board-certified veterinary dermatologist.

Managing allergies is lifelong, but most Frenchies live comfortably with proper treatment.
