## What to Look For

### Essential Coverage
- Hereditary and congenital conditions (most important for Frenchies)
- Chronic conditions (allergies need ongoing treatment)
- Emergency and specialty care
- Surgery and hospitalization
- Prescription medications
- Diagnostic testing (X-rays, MRI, blood work)

### Optional Add-Ons Worth Considering
- Preventive/wellness care (vaccines, dental cleaning)
- Physical therapy and rehabilitation
- Alternative therapies (acupuncture, chiropractic)

## What Is Typically Not Covered

- Pre-existing conditions
- Breeding costs
- Preventive care (on basic plans)
- Cosmetic procedures (tail docking, ear cropping)
- Exam fees (on some plans)

## When to Get Insurance

Get insurance **before** your Frenchie develops any health issues. Pre-existing conditions are not covered. The ideal time is the day you bring your puppy home.

## Cost-Saving Tips

3. Consider annual vs. per-incident deductibles
4. Look for multi-pet discounts if you have other pets
5. Read the fine print on hereditary condition exclusions
