# French Bulldog Myths Debunked

There are many myths about French Bulldogs. Here is the truth behind the most common misconceptions.

## Myth 1: French Bulldogs Cannot Swim

**True.** This is actually correct — but the reason matters. French Bulldogs are not natural swimmers due to their heavy heads, short legs, and flat faces. They sink rather than float. A Frenchie can drown in seconds in shallow water. Always use a life jacket near any water.

## Myth 2: All Frenchies Have Breathing Problems

**Partially true.** While most Frenchies have some degree of BOAS, not all have severe breathing issues. Well-bred Frenchies with proper airway conformation can breathe reasonably well. However, all Frenchies are at risk for overheating and should never be overexerted in hot weather.

## Myth 3: French Bulldogs Are Lazy

**False.** Frenchies are not lazy — they have moderate energy. They enjoy play sessions, walks, and exploring. They simply tire faster than other breeds due to their breathing limitations. A healthy Frenchie is playful and engaged.

## Myth 4: French Bulldogs Are Easy to Train

**False.** Frenchies are stubborn and selective learners. They understand what you want; they choose whether to comply. Training requires patience, high-value treats, and positive reinforcement. They are trainable but not eager-to-please like Golden Retrievers.

## Myth 5: French Bulldogs Are Hypoallergenic

**False.** No dog is truly hypoallergenic. Frenchies shed moderately and produce dander like any other dog. Their short coat does not reduce allergens. If you have allergies, spend time around Frenchies before committing.

## Myth 6: Rare Colors Are Healthier

**False.** Blue, lilac, merle, and other rare colors are linked to health problems. The gene that creates these colors is also associated with skin issues, alopecia, and immune disorders. Ethical breeders focus on standard colors.

## Myth 7: French Bulldogs Are Good Apartment Dogs Because They Do Not Need Walks

**Partially true.** Frenchies are excellent apartment dogs — but they still need daily walks. Walks provide mental stimulation, socialization, and exercise. Skip walks and your Frenchie will become overweight and destructive.

## Myth 8: Male Frenchies Are Friendlier Than Females

**False.** Personality varies by individual, not gender. Both males and females can be affectionate, stubborn, and playful. Temperament is influenced more by breeding, socialization, and training than by gender.

## Myth 9: French Bulldogs Are Dangerous With Children

**False.** French Bulldogs are one of the best breeds for families with children. They are sturdy, patient, and affectionate. They tolerate gentle handling better than many small breeds. Always supervise interactions between dogs and young children.

## Myth 10: A Snorting Frenchie Is a Healthy Frenchie

**False.** While some snorting is normal, excessive snorting, snoring, or gagging indicates airway obstruction. Heavy snoring is not cute — it is a sign of BOAS that may need veterinary attention. Frenchies should breathe relatively quietly when resting.

## Myth 11: French Bulldogs Cannot Wear Collars

**True.** Frenchies should use harnesses, not collars. Their tracheas are fragile and prone to collapse. Pulling on a collar can cause irreversible tracheal damage. Use a well-fitted harness for all walks.

## Myth 12: All Frenchies Love Cuddling

**Mostly true.** Frenchies are Velcro dogs that love being close to their owners. But individual personalities vary. Some Frenchies prefer their own space. Respect your dog's preferences — forced cuddling causes stress.

Knowing the truth helps you provide better care for your Frenchie. When in doubt, ask your veterinarian — not the internet.
